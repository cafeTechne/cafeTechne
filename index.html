A Philosophy of Accessibility

When nobody is left behind, we all move foreward together!

In this day and age, equal access to information is mission essential. Regardless of individual disposition, everyone should have equal access to services and content. Although screen reader technology has come a long way over the years, many people fail to consider the needs of clients whom are visually impaired. Rates of visual impairment affect a substantial proportion of the older population in our country. Many designers and web design professionals fail to consider the needs of this large segment of the population, needlessly ostracizing a substantial proportion of stakeholders and potential clients.

In fact, if your organization plans on receiving grants or doing work with Federal, State and/or Local governments, you are mandated by law to ensure an equal opportunity to access your services. According to The Americans with Disabilities Act: "On September 15, 2010 the Department published final regulations revising the Departmentâ€™s ADA regulations, including the adoption of updated ADA Standards for Accessible Design (2010 Standards). The revised final rules went into effect on March 15, 2011. Compliance with the 2010 Standards was required on March 15, 2012..." Our promise to you is this: we will work our hardest to help your site and services be in compliance with the standards set forth by the law--not because it is mandated, but because it is the right thing to do.
